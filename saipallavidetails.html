Sai Pallavi was born on 9 May 1992 in a Brahmin family in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. Her hometown is Kotagiri in the Nilgiris district, Tamil Nadu. Born to Senthamarai Kannan and Radha, she has a younger sister, Pooja Kannan, who has also worked as an actress. Sai Pallavi is an Indian actress who was born on May 9, 1992, in Kotagiri, Tamil Nadu, India. She hails from a family deeply rooted in the world of medicine; her mother, Radha Kannan, is a trained physician, and her father, Senthamara Kannan, is an academic who teaches at the Senthamarai College of Arts and Science in Pachal, Tamil Nadu. Growing up, Sai Pallavi showed an early interest in dance, particularly classical forms like Bharatanatyam. She pursued her passion for dance by participating in various cultural events and competitions during her school and college days. Her dedication and talent in dance earned her recognition and admiration. After completing her schooling, Sai Pallavi went on to study medicine at the Tbilisi State Medical University in Georgia. Despite studying abroad, she continued to nurture her passion for dance and was actively involved in cultural activities at her university. In 2015, Sai Pallavi made her acting debut in the Malayalam film "Premam," directed by Alphonse Puthren. Her portrayal of Malar, a young lecturer, earned her widespread acclaim and established her as a promising actress in the South Indian film industry. Following the success of "Premam," Sai Pallavi continued to appear in films across different languages, including Tamil and Telugu. Her performances in films like "Kali," "Fidaa," "Maari 2," and "Athiran" further solidified her reputation as a versatile actress capable of portraying a wide range of characters. Apart from her acting career, Sai Pallavi remains deeply committed to her academic pursuits. She completed her medical studies and graduated as a doctor. Despite her busy schedule in the film industry, she continues to maintain a balance between her passion for acting and her professional aspirations in the field of medicine. Sai Pallavi's success in the film industry is not just limited to acting; she is also known for her philanthropic activities. She has been actively involved in various social causes and has used her celebrity status to raise awareness about issues such as women's empowerment and education. In addition to her acting and dancing skills, Sai Pallavi is admired for her down-to-earth personality and simplicity. She has garnered a large fan following across South India and continues to be a popular figure in the film industry. Overall, Sai Pallavi's journey from a talented dancer and medical student to a successful actress is a testament to her determination, versatility, and commitment to both her artistic and academic endeavors. The year 2017 marked her debut in Telugu with Sekhar Kammula's Fidaa, in the role of Bhanumathi, a feisty village girl from Telangana.Her performance in the film is regarded as one of the "100 Greatest Performances of the Decade" by Film Companion.[36] She went onto win her first Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Telugu. Then she starred in the 2017 Telugu action comedy film Middle Class Abbayi alongside Nani, the film was a critical and commercial success. Her next release, Tamil–Telugu bilingual film Diya had an average run at the box office.Later, she starred in the Tamil film Maari 2, a sequel to Maari (2015), opposite Dhanush, directed by Balaji Mohan.A song from the film, "Rowdy Baby", set a record by becoming the first south Indian and Tamil song to clock over 1.5 Billion views on YouTube. Pallavi started shooting in February 2018 for the film Padi Padi Leche Manasu with Sharwanand,which was a huge commercial failure.In December, several news outlets reported that she refused to accept her full remuneration, expressing solidarity with the producers for the failure of the film. In 2019, she played Nithya Lakshmi, a non-verbal autistic character, in the 2019 psychological Malayalam thriller Athiran opposite Fahadh Faasil.Her next film in Tamil with actor Suriya, directed by Selvaraghavan titled NGK, released on 31 May 2019. In 2020, she was recognised by Forbes magazine as one of India's 30 under 30. She was the only person from film industry in that list.She also acted in a Netflix anthology film series Paava Kadhaigal segment Oor Iravu directed by Vetrimaaran. In 2021, she starred in the romantic drama Love Story directed by Sekhar Kammula alongside Naga Chaitanya in her second collaboration with Sekhar Kammula after Fidaa (2017) and Shyam Singa Roy opposite Nani in their second collaboration after MCA where she portrayed Maithreyi, a captivating devadasi in 1970s Kolkata. Her performances in both Love Story and Shyam Singha Roy earned her the Critics Award and the Best Actress award at the Filmfare Awards South. Sai Pallavi, the talented Indian actress, has garnered widespread acclaim for her remarkable performances across various film industries. She rose to prominence with her debut role as Malar in the Malayalam film "Premam" (2015), which not only earned her critical praise but also established her as a promising talent in the industry. Her portrayal of Malar won her several awards, including the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut – South and the SIIMA Award for Best Debut Actress. Following her successful debut, Sai Pallavi continued to impress audiences with her acting prowess in films like "Kali" (2016) and "Fidaa" (2017). In "Fidaa," she played the role of Bhanumathi, a Telangana village girl, which became one of her most memorable performances. The film was a commercial success and further solidified her reputation as a versatile actress. For her role in "Fidaa," she received the Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Telugu. Sai Pallavi's commitment to portraying diverse and challenging roles has earned her accolades not only in Malayalam and Telugu cinema but also in Tamil cinema. Her performances in Tamil films like "Maari 2" (2018) and "NGK" (2019) showcased her ability to adapt to different genres and characters. She has garnered a loyal fan base due to her natural acting style, expressive eyes, and ability to bring authenticity to her roles. In addition to her acting skills, Sai Pallavi is admired for her dancing abilities, which she has showcased in various films. Her dance performances, particularly in the song "Rowdy Baby" from "Maari 2," have become hugely popular and have contributed to her widespread appeal. Throughout her career, Sai Pallavi has been recognized with several awards, including Filmfare Awards, SIIMA Awards, and Behindwoods Gold Medals.
